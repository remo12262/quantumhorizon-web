The Next Evolution: Knowledge Graph Intelligence

Building six platforms taught us something fundamental: the most important insights don't live inside a single dataset. They emerge from connections between datasets — from understanding how a semiconductor company's financing chain creates cryptographic vulnerabilities in Italian infrastructure, or how a lobbying group's media relationships shape health policy decisions.

This insight led us to explore a new paradigm: knowledge graph intelligence, inspired by the approach pioneered by Lovelace AI's Elemental platform — a system that pre-computes billions of facts into a structured graph ready to support complex investigative reasoning.

"The question is never about individual data points. It's about the web of relationships between them — and what those relationships predict about what happens next."

We built our version of this: three new platforms that share a common architecture but serve radically different intelligence domains. Each one ingests data automatically from public sources every six hours, uses Claude AI to extract typed entities and relationships, computes risk and influence scores, and generates predictive alerts.

The Three New Platforms

Q-INTEL
Quantum & AI Security Intelligence Graph
New Quantum

Q-INTEL maps the global web of relationships between semiconductor companies, quantum computing labs, government agencies, investors, and sanctioned entities. The goal: identify supply chain risks that could compromise Italy's critical infrastructure before they materialize.

Every node carries a Risk Score (0–100). Every relationship is typed — COLLABORA_CON, FINANZIA, RISCHIO_PER, REGOLA — and carries its own risk assessment. Claude AI ingests news from Reuters, Wired, ArsTechnica, and ENISA feeds, plus live CVE data from the NVD and sanctioned entity lists from OpenSanctions.

What Q-INTEL detects today:

POLINT
Political Intelligence Graph
New Political

POLINT maps hidden influence networks across Italy, the European Union, and global geopolitics. Unlike traditional political monitoring tools that track what actors say, POLINT focuses on what they do — following financing flows, media control patterns, revolving door movements, and informal networks that shape policy decisions without appearing in official records.

Every relationship is scored on two axes: influence_score (how much this connection shapes political outcomes) and hidden_score (how opaque or undisclosed this connection is). The combination reveals what matters most: high-influence, high-opacity relationships — the ones that actually move policy.

What POLINT has already mapped:

  • 176 political actors across Italy, EU, and global networks
  • 276 typed relationships including FINANZIA_OCCULTO, RETE_INFORMALE, REVOLVING_DOOR
  • 24 predictive political alerts generated by Claude AI
  • 14 hidden influence networks identified and described
  • 51 high-opacity actors flagged for further investigation

The predictive engine generates forecasts with confidence scores, timeframes, and trigger event identification — answering questions like: which policy shift is most likely in the next 3 months, and which hidden network is driving it?

The Architecture Behind All Three

All three platforms share a common technical architecture that makes them easy to deploy, maintain, and evolve:

┌─ DATA INGESTION (every 6 hours, automatic) │ RSS feeds · Public APIs · OpenSanctions · ArXiv │ ├─ EXTRACTION ENGINE (Claude AI) │ Entity typing · Relationship classification │ Risk/influence scoring · Predictive alert generation │ ├─ GRAPH DATABASE (SQLite → upgradeable to Neo4j) │ Typed nodes · Typed edges · Score persistence │ └─ FRONTEND (React + HTML Canvas) Force-directed graph layout · Interactive node panel Multi-tab interface · Real-time data refresh

The choice of SQLite over Neo4j was deliberate for this phase: it eliminates infrastructure complexity while maintaining full graph query capability. The schema is designed for a zero-migration upgrade path to Neo4j AuraDB when scale demands it.

Claude AI serves as the extraction and reasoning engine throughout — not as a chatbot, but as a structured data processor that transforms unstructured news and documents into typed graph data and probabilistic forecasts.
